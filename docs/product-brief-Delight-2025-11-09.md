# Product Brief: Delight

**Date:** 2025-11-09
**Author:** jack
**Context:** Emerging self-improvement companion platform

---

## Project Identity

- **Name:** Delight
- **Naming Rationale:** Short, familiar word that signals warmth and effortlessness, countering perceptions of clunky AI productivity stacks.
- **Promise:** Software that feels seamlessly supportive and ruthlessly effective—guiding people through difficult emotional moments while propelling them toward goals faster than conventional tools.

---

## Executive Summary

Delight is a self-improvement companion for ambitious people who routinely set audacious goals but lose momentum when stress, overwhelm, or competing obligations take over. The product pairs emotionally intelligent coaching with structured progress systems so users can translate aspiration into consistent action, creating compounding personal growth and carving out differentiated monetization models in the self-improvement space. The flagship experience deliberately modernizes nostalgic “poke”-style helpers into an emotionally aware coach that can sense isolation, hold real conversations, and—in ambitious future versions—introduce people to each other when connection matters most. Users never face big goals alone: every interaction is designed to feel more human than mainstream tools, turning a hesitant prompt into a full plan, a wobble into an encouraging nudge, and every win into RPG-inspired streaks or highlight reels that prove their effort is working.

### Initial Vision Snapshot

- **Project Level:** Level 2 (structured multi-artifact initiative)
- **Origin Story:** Built to keep high-potential people on track when overwhelm or stress derails their most ambitious plans.
- **Opportunity Lens:** Unlock long-term societal upside and differentiated business models by sustaining self-improvement momentum at scale.
- **First Wave Focus:** Early adopters are high-potential individuals who already chase large goals but admit they do not yet have a predictable system for staying on track under pressure.

## Initial Vision Highlights

1. **Emotionally aware companion:** Conversational AI that blends empathy, coaching, and light social matchmaking so people feel seen, supported, and—when ready—introduced to peers who can help unlock the next milestone.
2. **Momentum scaffolding:** Micro-quests, daily “choose-one-of-three” priorities, and cinematic highlight reels ensure progress feels tangible even when long-term outcomes lag and help users author an “alter-ego” storyline about the person they are becoming.
3. **Motivation intelligence:** Configurable nudges (in-app, SMS, email) plus optional context awareness bring users back gently when they disappear without feeling nagged, so the companion feels like a caring check-in rather than transactional alerting.
4. **Abstract goal navigation:** Handles ambiguous ambitions (move abroad, build relationships, change careers) by probing underlying motivations, mapping permissive action paths, and staying engaged in nuanced conversations other AI tools avoid.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Ambitious creatives, founders, and operators routinely hit productivity stalls triggered by stress-induced inertia, compulsive context switching, and cognitive overload. They often know exactly what to do, yet struggle to even start after a meal or break, slip into TikTok or random tabs within minutes of working, and become paralyzed by sprawling lists of obligations they cannot mentally sequence. The result is a persistent gap between intention and execution that compounds over time.

Their current systems—DIY Notion setups, habit trackers, ad-hoc accountability chats—demand constant upkeep and quickly fall out of sync with real life. Without a responsive partner that understands both their emotional state and their workload, they lack a reliable way to regain momentum when their brain feels flooded. Ambitious-yet-abstract goals (moving abroad, nurturing relationships, reinventing careers) feel especially unsupported because traditional tools only track quantifiable tasks.

### Problem Impact

- Progress spirals downward: each missed work block increases guilt and avoidance, making the next session even harder to start.
- Tangible losses accumulate: missed classwork, delayed launches, lost revenue opportunities, and abandoned creative output.
- Confidence erodes: people internalize these stalls as personal failure rather than a solvable systems problem, limiting long-term potential.
- Lack of micro-progress visuals convinces users “nothing is working,” prompting doomscrolling or avoidance loops that drain the little energy they have left.
- Without compassionate outreach—texts, emails, gentle reminders—people forget the tool even exists; when they return, it feels like starting over.
- 2024 Future Forum Pulse reports 42% of knowledge workers feel burned out by context switching, reinforcing how widespread this momentum drag has become.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

- **Deterministic helpers:** Most AI companions regurgitate generic advice, refuse nuanced topics because of rigid content filters, or cannot reason about messy life goals.
- **Heavy maintenance:** DIY Notion stacks and habit trackers demand constant grooming; they collapse the moment life gets chaotic.
- **Shallow gamification:** Points and badges rarely connect to users’ emotional motivations, so the “game” becomes background noise.
- **No human-feeling outreach:** Email reminders and push notifications lack the conversational tone—or channel flexibility—to feel like a caring check-in.
- **Limited social pathways:** Existing tools rarely facilitate introductions or shared rituals, so people remain isolated even when community would unlock progress.
- **Coaches are inaccessible:** Professional human coaching is effective but expensive and not available during real-time hesitation moments.
- **Peer accountability alone is unreliable:** Friends share encouragement but cannot offer always-on structure, leaving users stranded between check-ins.
- **Generic habit apps ignore emotion:** Checkbox tracking does not sense overwhelm or adapt plans when a user’s emotional state changes.

### Proposed Solution

Delight orchestrates a human-centered productivity loop built around three layered interactions:

1. **Optional calming ritual:** Users can opt into short grounding exercises (breathing cues, reflective prompts) that signal a transition from rest to focused action while reinforcing emotional safety.
2. **AI companion dialogue:** A persistent companion greets the user, asks how they feel, and infers workload context (pulling from remembered goals, recent tasks, and any linked plans such as `docs/delight-plan.md`). This is the single most important pillar: each conversation deepens mutual understanding so the companion can reorganize priorities dynamically and coach with empathy rather than nagging.
3. **Adaptive micro missions:** The companion decomposes overwhelming backlogs into flexible mini-quests. Unlike rigid Pomodoro timers, missions adjust in duration and intensity; a user pushing for a hard sprint can stack missions, while someone seeking gentle momentum can tackle a single 10-minute action.

As work begins, Delight stays in the loop: gentle nudges surface when the user drifts (through declared context or privacy-aware sensing of tabs/apps), the companion confirms whether an interruption is intentional, and the system reshapes the plan if energy drops. When priorities become ambiguous or motivation dips, it leans into deeper coaching—uncovering the underlying “why,” reinforcing benefits, and proposing smart incentives such as social accountability pings, light forfeits, or rewards to keep users moving. Consistency is celebrated through streaks, highlight reels, and narrative feedback loops inspired by the brainstorming session log (`docs/bmm-brainstorming-session-2025-11-08.md`), creating exponential trust between user and AI over time.

Core gameplay metaphors (Arena, Observatory, Commons) externalize progress into a “between fiction and nonfiction” world so accomplishments feel shareable without requiring a full social metaverse on day one. Optional configurable nudges (in-app, email, SMS) deliver compassionate accountability, while quantitative dashboards (streaks, “what’s working” analytics) help users see that their investment of time is compounding. Ambitious future versions extend the same relational intelligence outward—introducing people to each other when connection would unlock progress and letting users co-author alter-ego storylines that make introspection feel heroic rather than clinical.

### Key Differentiators

- **Emotion-first guidance:** Productivity tools focus on structure; Delight leads with empathy and grounding, then layers structure, ensuring the system responds to how a user feels in the moment.
- **Living relationship with the user:** Each AI dialogue compounds context (in line with the multi-tier memory architecture in `docs/delight-plan.md`), so advice becomes more precise instead of resetting every session.
- **Flexible accountability:** Users can stay fully solo, lean on AI nudges, or eventually connect with humans the assistant recommends—bridging the gap between isolated work and community without forcing either extreme.
- **Privacy-autonomy balance:** Context tracking (e.g., tab monitoring) is opt-in and transparent; the companion asks for signals rather than quietly surveilling, reinforcing trust.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

- **Ambitious multi-track operators:** Creators, founders, and high-performing professionals who set outsized goals yet stall when simultaneous obligations, stress responses, or emotional fatigue break their execution rhythm.
- **Language learners & polymath generalists:** People juggling multiple learning tracks who need a gentle companion to break down practice, track streaks, and celebrate micro-progress without feeling judged.
- **Self-actualization & relationship seekers:** Individuals pursuing hard-to-measure life transformations (moving abroad, finding a partner, redefining identity) who need nuanced coaching rather than prescriptive task lists.
- **Momentum-switchers:** Users with ADHD tendencies or context-switch-heavy roles who crave dopamine, rapid feedback, and compassionate outreach when they vanish.

### Secondary User Segments

- **Overloaded founding teams:** Context-switching between investors, product decisions, and team leadership; they need an emotionally aware assistant that keeps them honest about priorities without piling on guilt.
- **Ambitious students and freelancers:** Juggling classes, clients, or multiple side projects; they often lack access to paid coaching yet seek consistent momentum and encouragement.
- **Accountability pods & small cohorts:** Groups that want to anchor rituals, share progress, and hold each other gently accountable while letting the companion handle daily scaffolding.
- **Therapist/coach augmenters:** Professionals who want to extend support between sessions without adding manual workload.

### User Journey Snapshot

1. **Stuck moment:** User feels overwhelmed (late night doomscrolling or stalled morning) and remembers—or hears via community/ads—about Delight.
2. **Opening Delight:** The interface senses their emotional state through friendly check-ins; optional calming ritual sets intention.
3. **AI grounding dialogue:** Companion mirrors their feelings (“You’re carrying a lot; let’s pick one move together”), surfaces context from past missions, and offers a realistic micro mission.
4. **Micro mission execution:** User completes a 10–30 minute focused action; companion stays present, nudging gently if distractions arise.
5. **Celebration + push:** Instead of shallow gamification, the companion genuinely acknowledges progress, reflects what it means (“This unlocks the next interview follow-up”), and suggests an optional stretch mission so momentum keeps building without burnout.

## MVP Scope

### Release Overview

The MVP commits to a single-player but emotionally aware loop that proves Delight can sense isolation, co-create actionable missions, and keep people moving without feeling gimmicky. The focus is on four pillars: (1) a companion with deep memory, (2) collaborative goal modeling plus adaptive “choose one-of-three” prioritization, (3) cinematic highlight reels and streaks that make micro-progress visible, and (4) compassionate outreach (in-app/SMS/email) that feels like a caring friend instead of transactional notifications. Multiplayer rituals, automated matchmaking, and heavier RPG overlays wait until the core loop shows reliable retention and can run within the <$0.10/user/day infrastructure budget.

### Feature Priorities & Acceptance Criteria

| Feature | Priority | Acceptance Criteria |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Emotionally aware companion | **P0** | ≥90 % of guided sessions open with remembered context (goals, emotional cues) and conversational CSAT averages ≥4/5 in pilot interviews. |
| Collaborative goal modeling & tracking primitives | **P0** | ≥80 % of new macro goals are decomposed into micro-quests within the first week, each with clear next actions and value tags (growth/health/craft). |
| Priority triads & adaptive missions | **P0** | ≥70 % of active users maintain 4+ day streaks in their first two weeks and ≥75 % of missions stay within the user’s requested energy/time band. |
| Momentum storytelling (streaks + highlight reels) | **P1** | Highlight reels auto-generate for ≥60 % of completed quests with positive sentiment scores, and ≥50 % of users watch at least one cinematic recap per week. |
| Quantitative consistency dashboard (DCI) | **P1** | Daily Consistency Index surfaces week-over-week; ≥65 % of WAUs view the dashboard weekly and ≥50 % sustain DCI ≥0.6. |
| Compassionate nudges | **P1** | ≥50 % of silent users who receive a gentle SMS/email nudge re-engage within 24 hours, while opt-out rates remain <5 %. |

### Delivery Guardrails

- Keep hosting, inference, and memory-layer spend under <$0.10 per active user per day at MVP scale; leverage the Mem0 + Qdrant stack documented in `docs/delight-plan.md`.
- Treat SMS/email nudges as a scarce resource: tie sends to high-signal drop-offs so marginal communication cost stays predictable.
- Ensure narrative overlays always tie back to real accomplishments so the experience feels like an alter-ego journey, not a bolt-on minigame.
- Keep privacy opt-ins explicit; no covert tab/app tracking during MVP.

### Out of Scope for MVP

- Financial forfeits or binding accountability contracts.
- Deep screen-time or app-tracking instrumentation (may return later as beta).
- Full multiplayer/social world spaces (Arena, Commons, etc.).
- Advanced community rituals, guild economies, or coin systems.
- Automated human introductions/matchmaking; run manual experiments only if necessary.
- Voice input, sprite rendering, or polished gaming UX beyond lightweight overlays.

### Future Vision

1. **Social world-building:** Unlockable RPG zones (Arena, Commons, Observatory) where progress unlocks shared rituals and accountability pods.
2. **Advanced accountability levers:** Optional financial stakes, buddy systems, and AI-mediated feedback loops for users who want firmer pressure.
3. **Emotion-aware sensory inputs:** Screen-time signals, voice tone, and wearable integrations that let the companion sense energy dips responsibly.
4. **Autonomous agent network:** Delegated agents that handle research, logistics, and orchestration so users stay in flow on meaningful work.
5. **Lore-rich economies:** Shared currencies, artifacts, and storylines that make long-term commitment feel like writing an alternate ego saga tied directly to real-world effort.

## Success Metrics

### User Success Measures

- **Daily Consistency Index (DCI):** Weighted blend of streak, quest completion, and accountability touchpoints; target ≥65 % of WAUs sustaining DCI ≥0.6.
- **Weekly quest completion rate:** ≥75 % of assigned micro-quests completed within seven days for primary personas.
- **Goal decomposition coverage:** ≥80 % of macro goals converted into collaborative micro-step plans with utility-weighted estimates inside the first week.
- **Narrative momentum score:** Each active user experiences ≥2 meaningful highlight reels or story beats per week with positive sentiment.
- **Companion engagement depth:** Median of ≥4 purposeful exchanges per session (goal modeling, reflection, reprioritization) with satisfaction ≥4/5.
- **Time invested in meaningful goals:** Track cumulative focused minutes per user per week to confirm Delight is increasing productive work time.
- **Emotional state trend:** Capture self-reported mood or cognitive load before/after sessions; improvements indicate the companion is reducing overwhelm.
- **Context switching reduction (background metric):** Monitor tab/app hopping behavior privately (if users opt in) to validate that guidance keeps them anchored, even if we do not surface it directly to them.

### Business Objectives

- **Week 1–2 retention:** Target ≥65 % Day-7 retention and ≥50 % Day-14 retention for core personas to prove the onboarding loop relieves early friction.
- **Trial-to-paid conversion:** Achieve ≥25 % conversion from generous trials to paid plans driven by perceived value, not scarcity pressure.
- **Activation quality:** ≥85 % of new users complete their first micro mission within 48 hours and self-report a positive mood delta afterward.
- **Day-30 retention:** Maintain ≥40 % Day-30 retention for target personas vs. baseline tools.
- **Planning quality differentiation:** ≥70 % of surveyed users state Delight-generated plans are “more actionable” than their prior system.
- **Motivation lift:** ≥50 % of users cite a moment when Delight nudged them through a task they would have otherwise abandoned.

### Leading Indicators

- **Organic word-of-mouth:** Unprompted community mentions, social posts, or referrals.
- **Testimonials referencing emotional relief:** Users sharing that Delight helped them “finally start” or “stay consistent” are early signs we’re solving the right problem.
- **Community buzz among high-intent groups:** Engagement in ADHD, founder, and student communities shows we resonate with the intended segments.

### Key Performance Indicators

- D1/D7/D30 retention segmented by persona and habit category.
- Average number of collaborative planning sessions per user per week.
- % of abstract goals (relocation, relationships, identity shifts) receiving mixed qualitative + quantitative plans.
- Highlight reel completion rate and shareability (exports or shares).
- Net Companion Score (qualitative trust rating in the AI companion).

### MVP Success Criteria

- **Mission completion by Day 3–7:** A meaningful share of new users complete at least one mission by day three and continue doing so through day seven.
- **Perceived goal progress:** Post-session check-ins show users feel more satisfied with their progress compared to prior tools.
- **Unique hook resonance:** Onboarding surveys/interviews confirm the narrative premise (emotion-aware companion + narrative world) is compelling enough to keep exploring after first contact.
- **Operational benchmark:** Companion collaborates on ≥80% of new goals without human intervention, and ≥70% of actives maintain 4+ day streaks in their first two weeks.
- **Content effectiveness:** Highlight reels auto-generate for ≥60% of completed quests with positive sentiment feedback, and core personas rate the planning flow ≥4/5 in interviews.

## Supporting Context

### Market Analysis

- Productivity tools remain polarized between heavy configuration platforms (Notion, Jira) and lightweight checklists (Apple Notes); neither pairs emotional intelligence with gameplay loops (reference: `docs/delight-plan.md` Executive Summary).
- Gamification data indicates 90% of employees report higher productivity when game mechanics are present, but most apps lack authentic emotional engagement, leaving a gap for Delight’s companion-first model.
- Personas such as the overwhelmed developer, ADHD professional, and busy founder (documented in `docs/delight-plan.md`) highlight demand for AI that understands context without manual setup.
- AI productivity offerings are crowded yet shallow: most assistants focus on summarization or task execution, not emotional coaching or abstract goal modeling.
- Competitive set spans Habitica (RPG productivity), Goblin Tools (neurodivergent planning aids), coach marketplaces, and accountability groups—none blend empathetic AI, quantitative rigor, and narrative worlds.
- Learners and technologists actively seek community-backed accountability but distrust platforms that feel like surveillance; Delight’s lore-first framing keeps the tone compassionate while still data-informed.

### Technical Preferences

- Adopt a three-tier memory stack (personal, project, task) built on Mem0 + Qdrant with LangChain utilities for short-term context, as laid out in `docs/delight-plan.md`.
- Hybrid retrieval strategy mixing knowledge graphs (NetworkX/Neo4j) with vector search ensures the companion recalls relevant goals and emotional cues efficiently.
- Application stack: FastAPI backend (packages/backend) orchestrating workflows + memory, Next.js frontend (packages/frontend) for immersive UI, shared TypeScript types for consistency.
- AI orchestration: LangChain/LangGraph, Mem0 for persistence, Redis for working memory, with optional MemGPT/Letta upgrade after MVP.
- Knowledge graph + analytics: NetworkX prototype scaling to Neo4j; quantitative metrics stored in Postgres powering the Consistency Dashboard.
- Experience layer blends AI coaching, mission generator, configurable nudges, and narrative overlay; future upgrades may include MemGPT-style hierarchical memory once MVP stabilizes.

### Risks and Assumptions

- **Product experience risk:** Technology exists, but user delight hinges on conversation design, emotional attunement, and avoiding superficial gamification.
- **Adoption assumption:** Users will trust an AI companion with emotional check-ins; opt-in privacy controls must be explicit to sustain that trust.
- **Motivation balance:** Need to celebrate wins without encouraging premature stopping—stretch missions and narrative framing must keep momentum without burnout.
- **Risk:** Quantitative tracking could feel punitive; mitigation is user-controlled visibility and positive framing.
- **Risk:** Narrative system might read as gimmick; mitigation is tying each story beat to verifiable work artifacts (quests, reflections, streaks).
- **Dependency risk:** Reliance on LLM providers, Mem0 infra, and SMS/email gateways could introduce outages; mitigation is multi-provider fallbacks plus graceful degradation to core chat/mission flows.
- **Assumption:** Target personas will opt into SMS/email nudges if tone remains human and respectful.

### Financial Considerations

- Post-MVP monetization: subscription tiers unlocking advanced rituals, co-op pods, and additional companion personas.
- Infrastructure costs anchored in Mem0 + Qdrant + Postgres stack, targeting <$0.10 per active user per day at MVP scale.
- SMS/email nudges incur marginal cost, so they remain tied to high-signal events until retention impact is proven.

### Organizational Context

- Founder-led initiative with direct access to research backlog (`docs/delight-plan.md`) and brainstorming outcomes.
- Requires close collaboration among AI/platform engineering (memory + orchestration), product design (narrative UX, rituals), and growth (accountability programs).
- Documentation centralized in `docs/` with BMAD workflows for traceability when additional contributors join.

### Timeline

- **Weeks 0–2:** Stand up companion + memory stack; ship conversational goal-modeling prototype.
- **Weeks 3–4:** Layer priority triads, streak/DCI tracking, and highlight reel generation.
- **Weeks 5–6:** Add configurable nudges (email/SMS), polish narrative zones, run retention experiments with pilot cohort.
- **Weeks 7–8:** Harden analytics, prep PRD workflow handoff, and evaluate expansion scope (screen-time beta, social pods).
